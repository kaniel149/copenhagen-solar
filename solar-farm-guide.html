Grid Vulnerability

Single 115kV submarine cable from mainland via Koh Samui. 2023: navy ship anchor damaged cable → blackouts on both islands. Peak season shortages (April-May). Grid upgrade to 230kV planned for 2028-2029.

Solar + Storage = grid stability insurance

Demand Growth

Tourism-driven electricity demand growing 5-8%/year. Full Moon Party draws 30K+ visitors monthly. Hotels, resorts, bars = high AC consumption. Island grid at capacity during peak.

Local generation reduces submarine cable dependency

High Electricity Costs

PEA rate: ฿5-7/kWh (among highest in Thailand). Island premium due to transmission losses. Businesses pay ฿7-9/kWh peak. PPA at ฿4.50 = immediate 25-40% savings.

Government Push

NEPC approved 1,500 MW community solar (Oct 2025). VSPP program: up to 10 MW per site. Factory license exemption for solar. Thailand targeting 64 GW renewables by 2037.

Regulatory Framework

VSPP Classification (≤10 MW)

Our 9 MW farm falls under Very Small Power Producer (VSPP) — the most accessible tier for solar development in Thailand.

Category Capacity Regulator Grid Buyer

Key Regulations (2025-2026)

Community Solar Framework (NEPC)

• 1,500 MW national target
• Ground-mount, max 10 MW/site
• One VSPP per grid connection point
• One project per community
• 25-year non-firm PPA
• FiT: ฿2.25/kWh (from PEA)

Self-Consumption Model

• No FiT — sell directly to customer
• PPA rate: ฿4.50/kWh (25% discount)
• No Ror. Ngor. 4 factory license needed (2025+)
• Simpler permitting, faster timeline
• Better economics than FiT
• Our preferred model

⚠️ BOI Alert (Important!)

BOI investment promotion for solar-only generation ended June 30, 2025. No 8-year CIT exemption for new solar farms. Alternative: hybrid models, or apply corporate tax deduction (50% of high-efficiency equipment costs, valid until Dec 2028).

Foreign Ownership

Foreigners (>49% foreign shares) cannot own land directly. Options:

Structure Pros Cons
Thai majority company (51/49) Can own land, standard approach Need trusted Thai partners
Long-term lease (30yr + 30yr) No Thai partner needed No land ownership, renewal risk
BOI certificate (if eligible) 100% foreign ownership + land Solar-only no longer eligible

03 · Permits

Approval Process — Step by Step

1. Land Acquisition & Title Verification

Secure Chanote (Nor. Sor. 4) title deed. Verify zoning allows energy/industrial use. Agricultural land OK under agrivoltaic rules (up to 23-year installations).

Timeline: 1-3 months

2. LAO Community Consent

Submit ERC-specified consent form to Local Administrative Organization (อบต./เทศบาล). Community hearing may be required. One project per community.

Timeline: 1-2 months

3. Environmental Review (IEE)

Solar farms typically need Initial Environmental Examination (IEE), not full EIA. Covers land use, drainage, visual impact. Near Than Sadet National Park = extra scrutiny.

Timeline: 2-4 months

4. ERC License Application

Apply for VSPP license from Energy Regulatory Commission. Submit: technical design, financial plan, site readiness proof, LAO consent, IEE report.

Timeline: 2-3 months

5. PEA Grid Connection Agreement

Apply to PEA for grid connection. Technical assessment of local grid capacity. One VSPP per connection point. 33kV or 22kV interconnection.

Timeline: 2-4 months

6. Construction Permits

Building permit from local authority. Structural engineering approval for mounting systems. Electrical installation permit. Road access permits if needed.

Timeline: 1-2 months

7. PPA Execution

Sign Power Purchase Agreement with PEA (FiT model) or directly with customers (self-consumption). 25-year PPA for FiT; flexible for private PPA.

Timeline: 1 month

8. Construction

Site preparation, foundation, mounting, panel installation, inverter/transformer, BESS, grid interconnection, testing.

Timeline: 6-9 months

9. Commissioning & COD

PEA inspection and testing. Grid synchronization test. Commercial Operation Date (COD). Must achieve within 18 months of PPA signing.

Timeline: 1-2 months

Total Timeline: 14-22 months from land acquisition to COD

Critical path: LAO consent + PEA grid assessment (can run in parallel with IEE). Construction starts ~month 8-10 after all permits secured.

Planned Upgrade New 230kV submarine cable — 2028 (Circuit 1) / 2029 (Circuit 2)

Grid Connection Options for 9 MW

Option A: 33kV Direct

• Connect at 33kV to local distribution
• Step-up transformer: 0.4kV → 33kV
• Shorter distance if near existing line
• Cost: ฿200K-500K per km
• Preferred for sites < 2km from line

Option B: Substation Direct

• Dedicated line to 115kV substation
• Higher capacity, more reliable
• Cost: ฿500K-1M per km
• Only practical for Thongsala South site
• Best for full 9 MW capacity

⚠️ Island Grid Constraint

9 MW on an island with 174 MW shared capacity is significant (~5%). PEA may require: grid stability study, frequency response equipment, mandatory storage. This actually strengthens the case for BESS — storage addresses PEA concerns AND creates additional revenue.

07 · Storage

Battery Energy Storage (BESS)

On an island with a vulnerable submarine cable, storage isn't optional — it's a competitive advantage.

🔋 BESS Specifications

Capacity: 4,500 kWh (50% solar:storage)
Power: 2,250 kW (2-hour discharge)
Technology: Huawei LUNA2000-215kWh × 21
Chemistry: LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate)
Cycles: 6,000+ (15-year warranty)
Round-trip Efficiency: 95%
Footprint: ~300 m² (containerized)

💰 Revenue Streams from Storage

1. Peak Shaving: Charge at midday (low demand), discharge at evening peak. Value: ~฿2.5M/yr
2. Arbitrage: Buy low (฿3/kWh off-peak) sell high (฿7/kWh peak). Value: ~฿3.3M/yr
3. Grid Stability: Frequency regulation for PEA. Value: ~฿1.0M/yr
4. Backup Power: Premium service during cable outages. Value: negotiable
Total BESS Revenue: ~฿6.8M/yr

Why PEA Will Favor Our Project (with BESS)

9 MW solar on a 174 MW island grid = 5% of capacity. Without storage, PEA may reject due to intermittency concerns. With BESS, we offer grid stability, peak shaving, and backup — making PEA an ally, not a gatekeeper.
