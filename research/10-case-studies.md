## 1. Thai Solar Energy PLC (TSE)

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2008
- **Listed:** SET (Stock Exchange of Thailand) — ticker TSE
- **Headquarters:** Bangkok, Thailand
- **Website:** thaisolarenergy.com
- **Market Cap:** ~฿5-8 billion (2025)

### Solar Portfolio
- **Total capacity:** 165 MW installed (136.1 MW proposed/contracted)
- **Total investment:** >฿4 billion (~$117 million)
- **Number of projects:** 21 ground-mount solar farms
- **Cost per MW:** ~฿24-29M/MW (installed capacity basis)

### Key Projects
| Project | Location | Capacity | FiT Rate | SCOD |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|------|
| TSE Roof 1-5 | Central Thailand | 5 × 5 MW | ฿5.66/kWh (Adder) | 2012-2014 |
| TSE Ground 1-8 | Nakhon Ratchasima | 8 × 8 MW | ฿6.50/kWh (Adder) | 2013-2015 |
| Phase 2 batch | Kanchanaburi, Lopburi | 21 projects | ฿2.17/kWh (FiT) | 2025-2027 |

### Recent Developments (2025-2026)
- Won 21 projects in Thailand's Phase 2 renewable PPA/FiT auction
- Total proposed capacity: 136.1 MW across ground-mount sites
- FiT rate: ฿2.1679/kWh for 25-year PPAs
- All projects classified as VSPP (≤10 MW each)
- Expected COD: 2025-2027 (phased)
- Positioned for Phase 3 auctions in 2025

### Financial Performance
- Revenue (2024): ~฿2.5 billion
- EBITDA margin: ~45-50%
- Project IRR: 10-14% (varies by vintage and FiT rate)
- Older projects (Adder ฿5.66-6.50) highly profitable
- Newer Phase 2 projects at ฿2.17 — tighter margins

### Lessons for TM Energy
- **Scale matters:** TSE's portfolio approach diversifies risk
- **FiT vintage:** Earlier projects with higher Adder rates are cash cows
- **Ground-mount economics:** ฿24-29M/MW all-in is realistic benchmark
- **Phase 2 FiT (฿2.17) is tight** — private PPA at ฿4.50 is much better economics

## 2. Kehua 10 MW — Tyre Factory Ground-Mount

### Project Details
- **Name:** Kehua Solar Farm
- **Location:** Rayong Province, Eastern Thailand (Eastern Seaboard industrial zone)
- **Capacity:** 10 MW (DC)
- **Type:** Ground-mount, fixed-tilt
- **Customer:** Tyre manufacturing factory
- **Model:** Self-consumption / private PPA
- **EPC:** Kehua Tech (inverter manufacturer) + local contractor

### Technical Specifications
- **Panels:** ~17,000 panels (580W class)
- **Inverters:** Kehua SPI series string inverters
- **Mounting:** Fixed-tilt ground-mount, 15° south-facing
- **Land area:** ~100 rai (~160,000 m²)
- **Grid connection:** 22kV to PEA distribution
- **Annual production:** ~13,500 MWh

### Financial Details
- **Total investment:** ~฿260-300M (~$7.5-8.5M)
- **Cost per MW:** ~฿26-30M
- **PPA rate:** Undisclosed (estimated ฿3.80-4.20/kWh)
- **Payback:** ~5-6 years (estimated)
- **Contract term:** 20 years

### Timeline
- Planning & permits: 8 months
- Construction: 6 months
- Commissioning: 2 months
- Total: ~16 months from start to COD

### Key Takeaways
- Industrial self-consumption model works well in Thailand
- Eastern Seaboard has strong grid and flat terrain
- Factory customers provide stable off-take (24/7 consumption)
- Kehua used own inverters — vertical integration advantage

## 3. Equator Solar One — 10 MW Farm

### Project Details
- **Name:** Equator Solar One
- **Developer:** Equator Solar (Thai-international JV)
- **Location:** Nakhon Ratchasima Province (Korat)
- **Capacity:** 10 MW (AC)
- **Type:** Ground-mount solar farm
- **Model:** FiT / PPA with PEA

### Technical Specifications
- **Panels:** LONGi mono PERC, ~17,500 panels
- **Inverters:** Huawei SUN2000 string inverters
- **Mounting:** Single-axis tracker (unusual for Thailand)
- **Land area:** ~80 rai
- **Annual production:** ~16,500 MWh (higher due to trackers)
- **Capacity factor:** ~18.8% (vs ~15% for fixed-tilt)

### Financial Details
- **Total investment:** ~฿350M (~$10M)
- **Cost per MW:** ~฿35M (higher due to trackers)
- **FiT rate:** ฿2.17/kWh (Phase 2)
- **Annual revenue:** ~฿35.8M
- **Payback:** ~10 years (FiT model)
- **25-year NPV:** ~฿180M

### Lessons for TM Energy
- **Trackers boost output 15-20%** but add ฿5-8M/MW cost
- **Fixed-tilt is better for island projects** (simpler, wind-resistant)
- **Korat region** has best irradiance in Thailand (1,700+ kWh/m²/yr)
- **฿2.17 FiT payback at 10 years** confirms private PPA is better model

- **Total capacity:** 400+ MW solar
- **Revenue (2024):** ~฿4.5 billion

### Key Project: Solar Farm Portfolio
- **36 solar farms** across Thailand (mostly 6-8 MW each)
- **Total:** 260 MW installed
- **Locations:** Nakhon Ratchasima, Sakon Nakhon, Lopburi
- **FiT rates:** Mix of Adder (฿8.00, ฿6.50) and FiT (฿5.66)
- **Land leases:** 25-30 year terms

### Financial Performance
- Average IRR (old projects): 18-22% (high Adder rates)
- O&M: In-house team, ~฿300K/MW/year
- Degradation: Outperforming warranties (0.3%/yr actual)

- SPCG proves long-term solar farming is extremely profitable in Thailand
- Their 36-farm approach = risk distribution
- Old Adder rates (฿6.50-8.00) were incredibly generous
- Current FiT (฿2.25) requires different financial thinking

## 10. Koh Phaluai — First Island Micro-Grid (EGAT)

### Project Overview
- **Location:** Koh Phaluai, Surat Thani Province (Gulf of Thailand)
- **Developer:** EGAT (Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand)
- **Type:** Island micro-grid — solar + BESS + diesel hybrid
- **Commission date:** 2019 (Phase 1), 2022 (expansion)
- **Population served:** ~1,000 residents

### Technical Specifications
- **Solar PV:** 1 MW ground-mount
- **Battery Storage:** 1.5 MWh lithium-ion (LFP)
- **Diesel generators:** 2 × 500 kW (backup)
- **EMS:** Intelligent energy management system
- **Grid:** Isolated island grid (no submarine cable)

### Investment & Costs
- **Total investment:** ฿172 million (~$5M)
- **Cost per kW (solar):** ฿172,000/kW = ฿172M/MW (including BESS + diesel + EMS)
- **Solar-only cost:** Estimated ฿30-35M/MW
- **BESS cost:** Estimated ฿50-60M
- **Annual O&M:** ฿5-8M

### Performance Results
- **Diesel consumption reduced:** ~60-70%
- **Renewable penetration:** ~40-50% of daily load
- **Grid stability:** Improved significantly with BESS
- **Blackout frequency:** Reduced by ~80%
- **CO2 reduction:** ~500 tonnes/year

### Challenges Encountered
1. **Monsoon season:** Reduced solar output Sep-Nov, increased diesel reliance
2. **Salt air corrosion:** Required marine-grade equipment
3. **Logistics:** All equipment shipped by boat — added ~20% to costs
4. **Battery degradation:** Higher than mainland due to heat + humidity
5. **Community acceptance:** Initially mixed — noise from diesel, visual impact of panels

### Lessons for TM Energy / Koh Phangan
- **Island micro-grid costs are 5-6x mainland** due to logistics + storage
- **60-70% diesel reduction is realistic** — not 100% elimination
- **BESS is essential** for island grid stability
- **Koh Phangan is different:** Has submarine cable = grid-connected option
- **Our advantage:** KP has grid backup, so we can do pure solar without diesel
- **฿172M for 1 MW is expensive** — but includes everything (BESS, diesel, EMS)
- **Pure solar on KP should be ฿26-35M/MW** (no diesel, shared grid BESS)

## 11. Koh Tao — Wave + Solar Hybrid

### Project Overview
- **Location:** Koh Tao, Surat Thani Province
- **Developer:** Pilot project — joint EGAT/DEDE/academic
- **Type:** Wave energy converter + solar PV + BESS
- **Status:** Pilot/demonstration phase
- **Population:** ~3,000 residents + ~300,000 tourists/year

### Koh Tao Grid Challenges
- No submarine cable — fully diesel-dependent
- 4 diesel generators: total ~8 MW capacity
- Diesel cost: ฿8-12/kWh (including logistics)
- Frequent shortages during peak tourist season
- Environmental concerns: UNESCO Global Geopark candidate

### Pilot System Specs
- **Wave converter:** Small-scale ocean wave energy (experimental)
- **Solar PV:** 500 kW ground-mount
- **BESS:** 1 MWh
- **Diesel offset target:** 20-30%

### Status & Results
- Wave energy component underperforming (technology still maturing)
- Solar component performing well — ~15% of daytime load
- BESS proving valuable for frequency regulation
- Full-scale rollout pending further funding and results

### Relevance to TM Energy
- **Koh Tao proves island solar demand exists** — even without grid
- **Wave energy is not ready** — pure solar + BESS is the play
- **Diesel at ฿8-12/kWh on isolated islands** = massive opportunity
- **Koh Phangan has the cable** — we don't need diesel backup
- **Tourist island dynamics:** Peak demand = peak solar production (summer)

## 12. Key Lessons Learned Across All Case Studies

### Financial Lessons
1. **FiT ≤ ฿2.25 is tight** — Private PPA at ฿4.50+ is 2x the revenue
2. **Early movers won** — Adder program (฿5.66-8.00) was incredibly profitable
3. **Project IRR ranges:** FiT 8-12%, Private PPA 14-20%, Industrial PPA 12-16%
4. **Payback:** FiT model 8-12 years, Private PPA 4-6 years
5. **O&M costs:** ฿150K-300K/MW/year (depends on scale and location)

### Technical Lessons
1. **Fixed-tilt preferred** in Thailand over trackers (simpler, typhoon-resistant)
2. **LONGi + Huawei** is the dominant stack (proven reliability, local support)
3. **BESS adds ฿18-25K/kWh** but unlocks grid services revenue
4. **Island installations cost 20-50% more** (logistics, marine-grade equipment)
5. **Degradation:** 0.3-0.5%/year actual (better than warranted 0.4-0.55%)

### Regulatory Lessons
1. **LAO relationships are CRITICAL** — start early, engage community
2. **BOI promotion** (if eligible) provides massive tax advantages
3. **ERC licensing** takes 2-4 months — plan accordingly
4. **Community solar framework** opens up island opportunities
5. **One VSPP per grid point** — be first, secure the connection

### Island-Specific Lessons
1. **Submarine cable = grid-connected advantage** (Koh Phangan)
2. **Diesel replacement is the strongest value proposition**
3. **Tourism demand aligns with solar production** (summer peak)
4. **Community engagement is MORE important on islands** (tight-knit)
5. **Marine-grade equipment is non-negotiable** (salt air corrosion)

## 13. Relevance to TM Energy / Koh Phangan Project

### How Our Project Compares

| Factor | TSE/SPCG (Mainland) | Koh Phaluai (Island) | TM Energy (KP) |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Grid | Mainland connected | Isolated | Submarine cable |
| Model | FiT ฿2.17-2.25 | Government-funded | Private PPA ฿4.50 |
| Cost/MW | ฿24-30M | ฿172M (all-in) | ฿32M (est. w/BESS) |
| IRR | 8-12% | N/A (public project) | 18-21% (projected) |
| Risk | Low | Low (EGAT) | Medium (off-take) |
| Advantage | Scale, FiT guarantee | Government backing | Premium PPA rate |

### Our Competitive Advantages
1. **Private PPA at ฿4.50 vs FiT at ฿2.25** — 2x revenue per kWh
2. **Grid-connected island** — no diesel backup needed, lower cost than Koh Phaluai
3. **Tourism-driven demand** — stable, growing off-take
4. **TM Developers partnership** — local entity, construction crew, client base
5. **First-mover on Koh Phangan** — no competing solar farms on the island

### Recommended Approach (Based on Case Studies)
1. **Start with 3-5 MW** (like community solar projects) — prove the model
2. **Target resort/hotel PPAs** (like Toyota/Solventia model)
3. **Include 2-3 MWh BESS** (like Koh Phaluai) — grid stability + premium
4. **Engage LAO early** (lesson from Super Energy's 47-project strategy)
5. **Use LONGi + Huawei stack** (proven across all Thai projects)
6. **Scale to 9 MW** in phase 2 after proving unit economics
