## 5. Government Agencies

### 5.1 Ministry of Energy (MoEN)
- **Role:** Oversees all energy policy in Thailand
- **Website:** https://energy.go.th
- **Contact:** 02-140-6000

### 5.2 Energy Regulatory Commission (ERC)
- **Role:** Regulates electricity procurement; sets Feed-in Tariffs (FiT); develops VSPP selection criteria; approves community solar regulations
- **Key for Solar:** FiT rates, VSPP licensing, grid connection approvals
- **Website:** https://www.erc.or.th
- **Contact:** 02-207-3599
- **Address:** 319 Chamchuri Square, Phayathai Road, Pathumwan, Bangkok

### 5.3 Energy Policy and Planning Office (EPPO)
- **Role:** National energy policy formulation; develops Power Development Plan (PDP) and Alternative Energy Development Plan (AEDP); oversees energy pricing
- **Key for Solar:** Community solar regulations (1,500 MW target, up to 10 MW/site), THB 2.25/kWh for 25-year PPAs
- **Website:** https://www.eppo.go.th
- **Contact:** 02-612-1555

### 5.4 Department of Alternative Energy Development and Efficiency (DEDE)
- **Role:** Promotes alternative energy and energy efficiency; implements AEDP; supports R&D
- **Key for Solar:** Alternative energy targets, technology standards, subsidies
- **Website:** https://www.dede.go.th
- **Contact:** 02-223-0021-9

### 5.5 Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand (EGAT)
- **Role:** State-owned; generates and transmits high-voltage electricity; manages national grid; plans power infrastructure
- **Key for Solar:** Grid integration, transmission infrastructure, 230 kV submarine cable projects
- **Website:** https://www.egat.co.th
- **Contact:** 02-436-0000
- **Note:** Does not distribute to end users — that's PEA and MEA

### 5.6 Provincial Electricity Authority (PEA)
- **Role:** Distributes electricity to all areas outside Bangkok/Nonthaburi/Samut Prakan; manages rural and island grids
- **Key for Solar:** Grid connection for solar farms in provinces; island infrastructure (Koh Phangan, Koh Samui, Koh Tao)
- **Website:** https://www.pea.co.th
- **Contact:** Call center 1129
- **Head Office:** 200 Ngamwongwan Road, Ladyao, Jatujak, Bangkok; Tel: 02-589-0100

### 5.7 Metropolitan Electricity Authority (MEA)
- **Role:** Distributes electricity in Bangkok, Nonthaburi, and Samut Prakan
- **Key for Solar:** Rooftop solar approvals in metro area, net metering
- **Website:** https://www.mea.or.th
- **Contact:** Call center 1130

### 5.8 Board of Investment (BOI)
- **Role:** Promotes foreign and domestic investment; grants tax holidays, import duty exemptions, land ownership rights for promoted activities
- **Key for Solar:** 
  - Category 7.1: Solar power generation — 8-year corporate income tax exemption
  - Import duty exemptions on machinery
  - Permission for foreign majority ownership in promoted projects
  - Direct PPA and third-party access facilitation
- **Website:** https://www.boi.go.th
- **Contact:** 02-553-8111
- **One Start One Stop (OSOS):** Investor assistance center

### 5.9 Department of Energy (DOE) / Department of Energy Business
- **Role:** Regulates energy business operations; issues licenses for fuel and energy businesses
- **Website:** https://www.doeb.go.th
- **Contact:** 02-794-4111

### 5.10 National Energy Policy Council (NEPC)
- **Role:** Chaired by the Prime Minister; sets national energy strategy and approves major energy plans (PDP, AEDP, Gas Plan, Oil Plan, EE Plan)
- **Key for Solar:** Final approval for FiT rates, community solar programs, PDP targets
- **Note:** Not a standalone agency — council of senior officials

### 5.11 Office of Natural Resources and Environmental Policy and Planning (ONEP)
- **Role:** Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approvals for power projects >10 MW
- **Website:** https://www.onep.go.th
- **Key for Solar:** EIA requirements for utility-scale solar farms

- **Note:** Specialist energy risk brokers connecting to Lloyd's markets

### Key Insurance Considerations for Thai Solar
- **Flood risk:** Critical — 2011 floods destroyed ground-mounted solar plants
- **Typhoon/storm:** Moderate risk in Gulf of Thailand
- **Equipment breakdown:** Inverter failure is top claim type globally
- **Business interruption:** Revenue loss during outages
- **Construction All-Risks (CAR):** Required during EPC phase
- **Third-party liability:** Required for grid-connected plants
