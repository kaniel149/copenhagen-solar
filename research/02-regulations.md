## 1. Regulatory Framework Overview

Thailand's electricity market is structured around three producer categories based on installed capacity:

| Category | Capacity | Regulator/Buyer | Notes |
|----------|----------|-----------------|-------|
| **IPP** (Independent Power Producer) | >90 MW | EGAT | Large-scale, competitive bidding |
| **SPP** (Small Power Producer) | 10–90 MW | EGAT | Firm/non-firm contracts |
| **VSPP** (Very Small Power Producer) | ≤10 MW | PEA / MEA | Simplified process, FiT-based |

For ground-mounted solar farms in the 1–10 MW range, the **VSPP framework** is the primary pathway. The Energy Regulatory Commission (ERC) oversees licensing, while the Provincial Electricity Authority (PEA) or Metropolitan Electricity Authority (MEA) serves as the power purchaser.

### Governing Legislation

- **Energy Industry Act B.E. 2550 (2007)** — Establishes ERC, licensing framework
- **Factory Act B.E. 2535 (1992)** — Factory classification (Ror. Ngor. 4)
- **Enhancement and Conservation of National Environmental Quality Act (NEQA) B.E. 2535 (1992)** — EIA/IEE framework
- **Foreign Business Act B.E. 2542 (1999)** — Foreign ownership restrictions
- **Investment Promotion Act B.E. 2520 (1977)** — BOI incentives
- **Revenue Code** — CIT, VAT, depreciation rules

### Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Role |
|--------|------|
| **ERC** (Energy Regulatory Commission) | Licensing, tariff regulation, grid access |
| **EPPO** (Energy Policy and Planning Office) | Policy, PDP planning |
| **NEPC** (National Energy Policy Council) | Strategic energy decisions |
| **PEA** (Provincial Electricity Authority) | Distribution, power purchase (outside Bangkok) |
| **MEA** (Metropolitan Electricity Authority) | Distribution, power purchase (Bangkok metro) |
| **DIW** (Department of Industrial Works) | Factory licensing |
| **BOI** (Board of Investment) | Investment promotion, incentives |
| **ONEP** (Office of Natural Resources and Environmental Policy and Planning) | Environmental assessments |

## 2. VSPP ≤10MW Framework

### 2.1 Definition and Scope

The VSPP program covers power producers with installed capacity **up to 10 MW** per connection point. For solar farms, this is the most accessible pathway.

**Key parameters:**
- Maximum capacity: **10 MW per site**
- One VSPP per grid connection point
- Power Purchase Agreement (PPA): **25-year non-firm** contract
- Buyer: PEA (provinces) or MEA (Bangkok metropolitan area)
- Tariff: Feed-in Tariff (FiT) as determined by ERC/NEPC

### 2.2 VSPP Sub-Categories

| Sub-Category | Description | Grid Connection |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **VSPP — Self-consumption** | Generate for own use, sell surplus | Net metering or feed-in |
| **VSPP — Power sale** | Primary purpose is selling to grid | Full feed-in |
| **VSPP — Community Solar** | Community-based, local consumption focus | Feed-in with community benefit |

### 2.3 Capacity Thresholds and Requirements

| Capacity Range | Factory License | ERC License | Environmental | Building Permit |
|---------------|----------------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| ≤10 kW (rooftop, self-use) | Exempt | Exempt | None | Exempt if <20 kg/m² |
| >10 kW to 1 MW (rooftop) | Exempt (Dec 2024) | Notification | None | Standard |
| >1 MW to 10 MW (rooftop) | Exempt (Dec 2024) | Full license | CoP/ESA | Standard |
| >1 MW to 10 MW (ground-mount) | Required* | Full license | IEE/CoP | Standard |

*Ground-mount factory license exemption proposed under Community Solar program but not yet confirmed as of March 2026.

### 2.4 Grid Connection Process for VSPP

1. **Pre-application**: Identify grid connection point, confirm capacity availability with PEA/MEA
2. **Technical study**: PEA/MEA conducts grid impact assessment
3. **Application**: Submit VSPP application with technical documents
4. **ERC review**: 30-day review period after document submission
5. **PPA negotiation**: Upon approval, negotiate 25-year PPA
6. **Construction**: Build within specified timeline (typically 2 years)
7. **Commissioning**: Grid testing, meter installation, commercial operation date (COD)

## 3. ERC Licensing — Step-by-Step

### 3.1 Pre-Application Phase

Before applying to ERC, developers must:

1. **Secure land rights** — Ownership, long-term lease (30 years typical), or usufruct
2. **Confirm grid capacity** — Contact PEA/MEA district office for capacity confirmation at the desired connection point
3. **Prepare technical design** — Single-line diagram, site layout, equipment specifications
4. **Engage local community** — For Community Solar projects, obtain Local Administration Organization (LAO) consent
5. **Environmental assessment** — Complete IEE or CoP as required (see Section 6)

### 3.2 Application Documents

**For Juristic Persons (Companies):**
- ERC application form (prescribed format)
- Company registration certificate (issued within 6 months, must list electricity generation as business objective)
- Authorization letter and authorized person's ID
- Evidence of electricity meter / connection point
- Land ownership or lease documentation
- Site plan and technical drawings (single-line diagram, layout)
- Environmental assessment report (IEE/CoP/ESA as applicable)
- Financial capability evidence (capital, bank guarantees)
- Implementation plan and timeline

**For Individuals:**
- Application form
- Copy of national ID card
- Electricity bill or meter number proof
- Building/land ownership evidence or owner consent

### 3.3 Review Process

| Step | Timeline | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1. Document submission | Day 0 | File at ERC office or branch |
| 2. Completeness check | Days 1–7 | ERC staff verify all documents present |
| 3. Deficiency notice | Day 7–14 | If incomplete, applicant has 30 days to cure |
| 4. Technical review | Days 7–30 | ERC Office evaluates technical feasibility |
| 5. Commission review | Day 30+ | ERC Commission considers Office recommendation |
| 6. Decision | Within 30 days of Office report | Approval, conditional approval, or denial |
| 7. License issuance | Post-approval | License issued with conditions |

### 3.4 Post-License Requirements

- **PPA development**: Negotiate and execute PPA with PEA/MEA within 2 years
- **Construction timeline**: Complete construction per approved schedule
- **Progress reporting**: Regular updates to ERC
- **Commissioning tests**: Grid compliance testing before COD
- **Ongoing compliance**: Annual reporting, safety inspections, tariff compliance

### 3.5 License Categories

| License Type | Applicability | Key Requirements |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Controlled Energy Production** | All power generators selling to grid | Technical specs, safety standards |
| **Energy Business Operation** | Commercial electricity business | Financial, technical, operational |
| **Energy Industry Safety** | Equipment and installation safety | Engineering standards compliance |

## 4. Community Solar Program (NEPC Oct 2025)

### 4.1 Program Overview

On **October 27, 2025**, the National Energy Policy Council (NEPC) approved a landmark **1,500 MW Community-Based Solar Power Generation Program** under the Ministry of Energy's **"Quick Big Win"** strategy.

### 4.2 Key Program Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Total capacity** | 1,500 MW nationwide |
| **Project size** | Up to 10 MW per site (VSPP classification) |
| **FiT rate** | Up to **฿2.25 per kWh** |
| **PPA term** | 25 years, non-firm |
| **Power buyer** | PEA or MEA |
| **Target communities** | 300+ communities nationwide |
| **Typical site size** | ~2 MW per community (matching local demand) |
| **Developer selection** | Based on readiness and technical capability |
| **Selection method** | First-come, first-served (if oversubscribed) |
| **Application period** | Expected December 2025 (ERC finalizing rules) |
| **Electricity purchase start** | November 2025 (initial plan, subject to ERC) |

### 4.3 Community Requirements

- **Local consent**: Must obtain approval from host community and Local Administration Organization (LAO)
- **One project per community**: Each local community limited to one project
- **One per connection point**: Single VSPP per grid connection point
- **Local benefit**: Electricity supplied to community at rates below retail tariffs
- **Community involvement**: Not just hosting — communities should benefit economically

### 4.4 Developer Requirements

- Demonstrate technical capability and readiness
- Financial capacity for project development
- Land access (ownership or long-term lease)
- Grid connection feasibility
- Track record in renewable energy (preferred but not mandatory for first round)

### 4.5 Economic Impact Projections

- **Total investment**: >฿30 billion (~$850M USD)
- **Job creation**: 1,600+ direct jobs
- **CO2 reduction**: 0.8 million tons/year
- **Energy cost savings**: Below-retail rates for host communities
- **Grid stability**: Distributed generation reduces transmission losses

### 4.6 Key Incentives

- Proposed **removal of Ror. Ngor. 4 factory license** requirement (Ministry of Industry tasked with implementation)
- Streamlined ERC licensing process
- Guaranteed FiT for 25 years
- Priority grid access for community projects

### 4.7 Status as of March 2026

- NEPC approval: **Confirmed** (Oct 27, 2025)
- ERC detailed regulations: **Under consultation** (not finalized)
- Factory license exemption: **Proposed** (Ministry of Industry reviewing)
- Application process: **Expected to open** (pending ERC finalization)

> **Note**: The 1,500 MW program supersedes earlier proposals for annual 400 MW community solar quotas.

## 5. Factory License Exemption (Ror. Ngor. 4)

### 5.1 Background

Under the Factory Act B.E. 2535 (1992), electricity generation facilities above certain thresholds were classified as "factories" requiring a **Ror. Ngor. 4 factory operation license** from the Department of Industrial Works (DIW). This historically applied to solar installations exceeding 1 MW.

### 5.2 December 2024 Exemption (Rooftop Solar)

**Ministerial Regulation Re: Designation of Type, Kind, and Size of Factories (No. 3) B.E. 2567 (2024)**

Effective **December 28, 2024**, this regulation exempted rooftop solar installations from Ror. Ngor. 4 requirements:

| Aspect | Pre-Dec 2024 | Post-Dec 2024 |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Rooftop solar >1 MW | Ror. Ngor. 4 required | **Exempt** |
| ESA report | Required with Ror. Ngor. 4 | **Not required** |
| Scope | N/A | All rooftop/building-mounted, any capacity |
| Location restriction | N/A | Outside industrial estates |
| Ongoing applications | Must complete | Can discontinue |

### 5.3 Scope of Exemption

**Covered (Exempt from Ror. Ngor. 4):**
- Solar PV on rooftops, roof decks, building surfaces
- Residential, commercial, and industrial buildings
- Any capacity (no upper limit)
- Outside industrial estates

**NOT Covered (Still require Ror. Ngor. 4):**
- **Ground-mounted solar farms** — Not explicitly covered by Dec 2024 exemption
- Installations within industrial estates (require IEAT permits, e.g., Land Use Permit IEAT 01/2)
- Non-solar power generation

### 5.4 Community Solar Exemption (Proposed)

Under the NEPC October 2025 Community Solar program, the Ministry of Industry has been **tasked with exempting** ground-mounted community solar projects from Ror. Ngor. 4. As of March 2026:

- **Status**: Under review by Ministry of Industry
- **Expected scope**: Ground-mounted solar ≤10 MW within Community Solar program
- **Impact**: Would significantly reduce permitting time and cost for solar farms
- **Timeline**: Not confirmed; dependent on Ministry of Industry action

### 5.5 Practical Implications for Solar Farm Developers

For ground-mounted solar farms **outside** the Community Solar program:
1. **Ror. Ngor. 4 still required** as of March 2026
2. Apply to DIW for factory classification and license
3. Prepare Environmental Safety Assessment (ESA) as part of application
4. Processing time: Typically 60–120 days
5. Annual renewal and compliance inspections required

## 6. Environmental Requirements: IEE vs EIA

### 6.1 Overview

Thailand's environmental assessment framework under NEQA provides multiple levels of review. Notably, **solar farms are generally NOT subject to full EIA** — they follow lighter assessment pathways.

### 6.2 Assessment Types

| Assessment | Full Name | Applicability | Rigor |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **EIA** | Environmental Impact Assessment | High-impact projects (dams, petrochemical, large hotels) | Full study, public hearing, ONEP review |
| **IEE** | Initial Environmental Examination | Medium-impact projects, some solar with transmission | Streamlined study, limited public process |
| **CoP** | Code of Practice | Standard renewable energy projects | Self-assessment against prescribed standards |
| **ESA** | Environmental Safety Assessment | Factory-classified facilities | Safety-focused, tied to Ror. Ngor. 4 |

### 6.3 Solar Farm Requirements

**Key finding: Solar farms in Thailand do NOT require full EIA.**

Solar projects typically require:

| Project Type | Assessment Required | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| Rooftop solar (any capacity, post-Dec 2024) | **None** | ESA removed with Ror. Ngor. 4 exemption |
| Ground-mount solar ≤10 MW | **CoP or ESA** | CoP if no Ror. Ngor. 4; ESA if factory-licensed |
| Ground-mount solar >10 MW (SPP) | **CoP/ESA** | Standard for SPP-class solar |
| Floating solar | **IEE** | Due to water body impact (e.g., Nam Phung Dam project) |
| Solar with 33 kV transmission line | **IEE** | Transmission infrastructure triggers IEE |
| Solar in protected areas | **EIA possible** | Near national parks, wetlands, coastal zones |

### 6.4 CoP Requirements (Most Common for Solar Farms)

The Code of Practice for solar farms typically covers:

1. **Site assessment** — Land use compatibility, topography, drainage
2. **Construction phase** — Dust control, noise, waste management, worker safety
3. **Operations phase** — Panel cleaning chemicals, vegetation management, wildlife impact
4. **Decommissioning** — Panel disposal, site restoration plan
5. **Monitoring** — Water quality, noise levels, community complaints
6. **Reporting** — Annual environmental compliance report to ERC

### 6.5 IEE Requirements (When Triggered)

If IEE is required (floating solar, transmission lines, sensitive areas):

1. **Baseline study** — Environmental conditions before project
2. **Impact prediction** — Potential effects on water, air, soil, biodiversity
3. **Mitigation measures** — How impacts will be minimized
4. **Public participation** — Community consultation (less extensive than EIA)
5. **Monitoring plan** — Ongoing environmental tracking
6. **Submission** — To ONEP or relevant agency for review

### 6.6 Practical Guidance

For a typical 5–10 MW ground-mount solar farm in rural Thailand:
- **Most likely requirement**: CoP (self-assessment) + ESA (if factory-licensed)
- **Timeline**: 30–60 days for CoP preparation
- **Cost**: ฿200,000–500,000 for environmental consultant
- **Risk areas**: Near waterways, forests, protected zones → may trigger IEE
- **Tip**: Engage environmental consultant early; they can confirm which assessment applies to your specific site

## 7. Foreign Business Act (FBA)

### 7.1 Overview

The Foreign Business Act B.E. 2542 (1999) restricts foreign ownership (>49% foreign equity) in certain business activities. Energy generation, including solar, falls under **List 3** restricted activities.

### 7.2 Key Restrictions

| Aspect | Restriction | Workaround |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Foreign equity** | Maximum 49% without FBA license or BOI | Apply for BOI promotion |
| **Land ownership** | Foreigners cannot own land | Long-term lease (30 years + 30 renewal), BOI land rights |
| **Business operation** | List 3 restrictions apply | BOI certificate allows full foreign ownership |
| **Nominee structures** | Illegal and actively enforced | Use legitimate BOI or JV structure |

### 7.3 Legitimate Structures for Foreign Solar Developers

**Option 1: BOI-Promoted Company (Recommended)**
- Apply for BOI promotion under renewable energy category
- Allows **100% foreign ownership**
- Includes land ownership rights
- 8-year CIT holiday + import duty exemptions
- Work permits and visas facilitated

**Option 2: Joint Venture (49/51)**
- Foreign party holds 49%, Thai partner holds 51%
- No BOI required but no tax incentives
- Requires trustworthy Thai partner
- Shareholder agreement critical for control provisions

**Option 3: FBA License (Rare)**
- Apply directly for FBA license from Ministry of Commerce
- Rarely granted for energy projects
- Long processing time, uncertain outcome

### 7.4 2025 FBA Amendments

In **April 2025**, the Thai Cabinet approved in principle amendments to the FBA:
- **Liberalization of select List 3 sectors** — Raising foreign ownership limits in certain activities
- **Signal of reduced protectionism** — Aimed at attracting more foreign investment
- **Solar-specific impact**: Not yet detailed; amendments are broad economic reform
- **Status**: Approved in principle; detailed legislation pending

### 7.5 Practical Recommendations

For Copenhagen Solar (Danish company entering Thailand):

1. **BOI promotion is the clear path** — Provides foreign ownership, tax benefits, and operational flexibility
2. **Apply for BOI before company registration** — BOI approval needed before setting up Thai entity
3. **Timeline**: BOI application to approval typically 60–90 days
4. **Legal counsel**: Engage Thai law firm with BOI experience (Tilleke & Gibbins, Baker McKenzie, Chandler MHM)
5. **Land**: Secure land option/MOU before BOI application; can own land post-BOI approval

## 8. BOI Investment Promotion (Post-June 2025)

### 8.1 Eligible Activities for Solar

The BOI promotes several solar-related activities:

| Activity | BOI Category | Tax Holiday | Import Duty |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Solar power generation | A1-A2 | 8 years CIT exemption | Exempt on machinery |
| Solar cell manufacturing | A1 | 8 years CIT exemption | Exempt on machinery |
| Energy storage (BESS) | A1-A2 | 8 years CIT exemption | Exempt on machinery |
| Solar raw material manufacturing | A1 | 8 years CIT exemption | Exempt on machinery |

### 8.2 Key BOI Incentives

**Tax Incentives:**
- **8-year Corporate Income Tax exemption** on profits from promoted activity
- **Import duty exemption** on machinery and equipment
- **25% tax exemption** on facility construction/installation costs
- **150% enhanced deduction** on new, 5-star rated energy-efficient equipment (must be operational by Dec 31, 2028)
- **Double deduction** on transportation, electricity, and water costs (for projects in promoted zones)

**Non-Tax Incentives:**
- **100% foreign ownership** permitted
- **Land ownership rights** for foreign-majority companies
- **Work permits** for foreign experts and technicians (streamlined process)
- **Visa facilitation** for foreign investors and employees
- **Permission to bring in skilled workers** — Not subject to normal quotas

### 8.3 BOI Application Process

| Step | Timeline | Details |
|------|----------|---------|
| 1. Pre-consultation | Week 1–2 | Meet BOI officials, confirm eligibility |
| 2. Application preparation | Week 2–4 | Complete BOI application form, business plan |
| 3. Application submission | Week 4 | File at BOI One Start One Stop (OSOS) center |
| 4. Review and site visit | Week 4–10 | BOI reviews application, may visit proposed site |
| 5. Board approval | Week 10–12 | BOI Board meets monthly to approve projects |
| 6. Promotion certificate | Week 12–14 | Certificate issued with conditions |
| 7. Company registration | Post-certificate | Register Thai company with BOI certificate |
| 8. Compliance | Ongoing | Annual reporting, milestone compliance |

### 8.4 BOI Conditions and Obligations

- **Minimum investment**: Typically ≥฿1 million (excluding land and working capital)
- **Feasibility study**: Required as part of application
- **Thai employment**: Must employ Thai nationals; ratios apply for foreign workers
- **Operational start**: Must commence operations within specified timeline
- **Reporting**: Annual reports on investment, employment, production
- **No double-dipping**: Cannot combine BOI tax holiday with other tax incentive schemes

### 8.5 Post-June 2025 Changes

The BOI has been updating its promotion policies to align with Thailand's carbon neutrality goals (2050 target):

- **Enhanced incentives for renewable energy** — Solar and BESS receive highest-tier (A1-A2) promotion
- **Streamlined processing** — Target 60-day approval for clean energy projects
- **Expanded scope** — Includes micro-grid, smart grid, and community energy projects
- **Alignment with PDP** — Power Development Plan targets 51% renewables by 2037

## 9. Tax Framework

### 9.1 Corporate Income Tax (CIT)

**Standard rates (without BOI):**

| Taxable Profit (THB) | Rate |
|----------------------|------|
| 0 – 300,000 | 0% (SMEs only) |
| 300,001 – 3,000,000 | 15% (SMEs only) |
| >3,000,000 | **20%** |

- Thai-incorporated companies are taxed on **worldwide income**
- Foreign companies are taxed on **Thai-sourced income** only
- Tax year follows accounting period (typically calendar year)

**With BOI promotion:**
- **8-year CIT exemption** on promoted activity profits
- After exemption period: Standard 20% rate applies
- Losses during BOI period can be carried forward 5 years

### 9.2 Value Added Tax (VAT)

| Item | VAT Rate | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Standard rate | **7%** | Applies to most goods and services |
| Solar equipment purchase | 7% | Must purchase from VAT-registered sellers |
| Electricity sales | 7% | Charged on electricity sold to PEA/MEA |
| Construction services | 7% | On contractor invoices |
| Import duty-exempt machinery | 0% VAT on import | If BOI-promoted |
| Export of electricity | 0% | If applicable |

**Key requirements:**
- Solar equipment must be purchased from **VAT-registered businesses** with proper **e-Tax Invoices**
- Input VAT on purchases can be offset against output VAT on electricity sales
- Monthly VAT filing required

### 9.3 Depreciation

**Standard depreciation rates for solar farm assets:**

| Asset Category | Useful Life | Annual Rate | Method |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Solar panels | 20 years | 5% | Straight-line |
| Inverters | 10 years | 10% | Straight-line |
| Mounting structures | 20 years | 5% | Straight-line |
| Transformers | 20 years | 5% | Straight-line |
| Electrical equipment | 10 years | 10% | Straight-line |
| Buildings/structures | 20 years | 5% | Straight-line |
| Vehicles | 5 years | 20% | Straight-line |
| Computer/SCADA | 3 years | 33.3% | Straight-line |

**Special provisions:**
- **Revenue Code Section 65 bis (2)**: Governs depreciation of eligible equipment
- **50% CIT deduction**: Available for new, high-efficiency machinery (including solar) — must be unused, located in Thailand, purchased from VAT-registered seller with e-Tax Invoice, ready for use by Dec 31, 2028
- **Cannot combine** 50% deduction with BOI tax holiday
- BOI-promoted projects: Standard depreciation applies during and after CIT holiday

### 9.4 Withholding Tax

| Payment Type | WHT Rate | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Dividends (to foreign company) | 10% | May be reduced by DTA |
| Interest (to foreign lender) | 15% | May be reduced by DTA |
| Royalties/service fees | 15% | May be reduced by DTA |
| Contractor payments (Thai) | 3% | Creditable against CIT |

**Denmark-Thailand Double Tax Agreement (DTA):**
- Dividends: 10% (if ≥25% ownership) or 15% otherwise
- Interest: 10%
- Royalties: 5% or 15%

### 9.5 Property and Land Taxes

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Land and Building Tax | 0.01–0.7% of appraised value | Agricultural land rate lower |
| Transfer fee | 2% of appraised value | On land purchase |
| Stamp duty | 0.5% of appraised value | On land transfer |
| Specific business tax | 3.3% | On commercial land sales within 5 years |
